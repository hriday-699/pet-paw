Prevention Of Cruelty To Animals Act.

(a) As per constitutional provision, management of stray animals is the mandate of local bodies of the State however, the Central Government has notified the Animal Birth Control Rules, 2023 vide G.S.R. 193(E) dated 10th March, 2023 under Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 after superseding the Animal Birth Control (Dog) Rules, 2001. As per Rule 3(1), the local authority may conduct the Animal Birth Control Program to reduce the stray dogs. As per Rule 9 (3) of the said Rules, the formation of Central Animal Birth Control Monitoring & Coordination Committee, State Animal Birth Control Implementation and Monitoring Committee and local Animal Birth Control Monitoring Committee are mandatory for effective implementation of Animal Birth Control Programs in accordance with these rules to control the population of street animals, for eradication of rabies and for reducing man-animal conflict. The Central Government circulated ABC Rules, 2023 vide letter dated 23.03.2023 to the Chief Secretary of all States/UTs, vide letter dated 27.03.2023 to the Principal Secretary of Department of Animal Husbandry and Principal Secretary of Department of Urban Development and vide letter dated 31.03.2023 to Municipal Commissioner of all District of all States to undertake the ABC Rules in letter and spirit. The Hon’ble Supreme Court of India in writ petition no. 691 of 2009 between Animal Welfare Board of India and People for Elimination of Stray Troubles, directs that "….Needless to emphasize, no innovative method or subterfuge should be adopted not to carry out the responsibility under the 1960 Act or the 2001 Rules. Any kind of laxity while carrying out statutory obligations, is not countenanced in law….". Hence, it is stated that the stray dog can only be managed by controlling their population through Animal Birth Control.(b) The direction of the Hon’ble Delhi High Court has been included in the Animal Birth Control Rules, 2023. As per Rule 20(1) of the ABC Rules, 2023, it shall be the responsibility of the Resident Welfare Association or Apartment Owner Association or Local body’s representative of that area to make necessary arrangement for feeding of community animals. As per Rule 20(2), an animal welfare committee shall be formed to resolve the conflict between the RWA or AOA and the animal caregivers or other residents. The Animal Welfare Board of India, on receipt of the various complaints from animal caregivers, individually requests the concerned RWA to form the animal welfare committee to resolve the dispute regarding feeding of community animals. After the Delhi High Court Order, the Board has so far issued 83 letters to the RWAs in the state of Delhi for constituting Animal Welfare Committee. (c) No, Sir (d) and (e) Yes, Sir. The Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying, GoI has released financial assistance to the states for the vaccination of stray dogs. Also, the Animal Welfare Board of India released the financial assistance for sterilization and immunization of stray dogs to the recognized Animal Welfare Organization(s) including Local Authority. Further, the AWBI had also released the fund received form NCDC, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare under National Rabies Control Programme (NRCP) which was implemented through Humane Society International (HSI) India, Mumbai, Worldwide Veterinary Services, Ooty and Friendicoes SECA, New Delhi in the state of Haryana.
